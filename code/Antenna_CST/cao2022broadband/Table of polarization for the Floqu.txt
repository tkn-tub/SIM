Table of polarization for the Floquet port modes

Receiver-Transmitter Antennas

-This design is a two-port structure, where the input port is placed at the layer M1 and the output port is at layer M5

Zmax: boundary at the larger z-coordinate, in this design, is the top layer M1
    Zmax(1) refers to mode 1
    Zmax(2) refers to mode 2
	
Zmin: boundary at the smaller z-coordinate, in this design, is the bottom layer M5


Polarization modes Incident Field with coordinate Zmax

Ingoing Field
Zmax In e1  = y-polarization
Zmax In e2  = x- polarization

Outgoing Field with Zmin
Zmin Out e1 = y-polarization
Zmin Out e2 = x-polarization


Zmin Out e1 ZMax In e2

There are also outgoing fields to Zmax wich refers to Zmax Out in CST, and also ingoing fields to M5, which refers to Zmin In.

Floquet port:
- For a Floquet port, CST decomposes the field into Floquet modes
	- incident modal waves
	- reflected modal waves
	- transmitted modal waves

S-parameters
- S-parameter is a ratio of power-normalized modal wave amplitudes defined as

S_ij = b_i / a_j 

where

a_i = incident wave amplitude entering port i
b_i = outgoing wave amplitude leaving port i
